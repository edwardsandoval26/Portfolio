## Problem Statement

Prostate cancer is the most commonly diagnosed cancer in men. Biparametric MRI (BP-MRI) is a powerful non-invasive imaging modality for detecting clinically significant prostate cancer (csPCa), but its interpretation is challenging due to the high variability between benign and malignant tissue patterns.

Most deep learning approaches require **large labeled datasets and expert annotations**, which are expensive and difficult to obtain in medical imaging. The goal of this work was to develop a **self-supervised representation learning method** capable of extracting discriminative MRI features while reducing dependence on labeled data.

## Dataset

- **Source:** PI-CAI public prostate MRI dataset  
- **Samples:** 1,295 patients  
- **Class distribution:**  
  - 1,075 non-csPCa  
  - 220 csPCa  
- **Modalities used:** T2W, ADC, and DWI MRI sequences  

Lesion-centered **32×32×12 volumetric regions of interest (VROIs)** were extracted and standardized before feature computation.

## Pipeline

![Pipeline](/images/projects/RIEMAE/riemae_pipeline.png)

**Step A.1 — Feature Extraction**  
MRI slices from each modality are processed using a pretrained **VGG19 CNN** to obtain feature activation maps.

**Step A.2 — SPD Descriptor Construction**  
CNN features are summarized using **covariance matrices**, producing compact **SPD descriptors** that capture pairwise feature relationships.

**Step B.1 — Self-Supervised Representation Learning**  
The proposed **RieMAE** learns compact embeddings directly on the SPD manifold using **BiMap**, **ReEig**, and a covariance masking strategy.

**Step B.2 — Classification**  
The learned SPD embeddings are projected to Euclidean space using a **Riemannian logarithm map**, vectorized, and used to train a **Logistic Regression classifier** to distinguish:

- Clinically significant prostate cancer (csPCa)
- Non-csPCa

## Comparison with State-of-the-Art

| Method | Accuracy | F1 | AUC-ROC |
|------|------|------|------|
| SPD + Classical ML | 91.04% | 77.46 | 93.53 |
| Supervised Contrastive Learning | 88.0% | 52.0 | 85.0 |
| SPDNet (Supervised) | 90.74% | 75.13 | 93.93 |
| 3DConv-SPDNet (Fully Supervised) | 90.80% | **79.82** | **96.68** |
| **RieMAE (Self-Supervised)** | **91.17%** | **77.91** | **94.29** |

Despite being **self-supervised**, the proposed RieMAE achieves **state-of-the-art accuracy and competitive F1 scores**, demonstrating that geometry-aware representation learning can effectively model MRI lesion patterns with limited labeled data.
